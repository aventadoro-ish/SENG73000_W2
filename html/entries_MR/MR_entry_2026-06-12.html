CAN Bus Cable Planning

NK and I planned how to solder the CAN bus cable using DB9 cables we got from the Tool Room.

An approach on how to split cables was devised. This approach attempts to balance finished cable quality, electrical insulation, and labour. The procedure is:

  1. Strip a segment of the insulation on the main bus cable and cut 3 conductors which connect to CANH, CANL, and Gnd lines
  2. Cut a small 20cm-long end of a cable that will branch off from the main bus
  3. Make 3 short wire segments, strip insulation from both ends and the middle
  4. Solder the wires from the short cable to the middle of the 3 wire segments as a T-joint
  5. Cover the T-joint with insulation
  6. Add thick heat-shrink insulation around the branching off cable to act as the strain relief on all 3 T-Joints, add short insulation to both ends of each of 3 wire segments
  7. Solder both ends of the 3 wires to the 2 cut wires from the main bus, such that the short segment joins the broken wires of the main cable
  8. Shrink the heat-shrink insulation

NK took over this task from me to let me develop the EC module during the weekend.
